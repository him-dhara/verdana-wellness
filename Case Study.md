## The Problem With Most Wellness Brands

Waaait... have you noticed how every wellness website looks the same??

Soft gradients. Stock photos of women meditating. Copy that says "we care" without ever proving it.

Nobody in real pain looks at that and thinks - *yeah, this is for me.*

That's exactly what Verdana was built to fix.

The mental health space is FLOODED with brands that are either too clinical or too soft.

Neither makes someone who's exhausted, burnt out, and skeptical feel safe enough to take the first step.

Verdana needed to thread that needle.

Warm but not weak.
Evidence-based but not cold.
Human but not unprofessional.

## The Brand System

Two colors. THAT'S IT. 🙃

| Role | Token | Hex |
|------|-------|-----|
| Light | Primary / Light | `#FBFFF5` |
| Dark | Primary / Dark | `#00545F` |
| Border | Primary / Border | `#00545F` at 10% |

No warm tones sneaking in. No "just one more accent."

Two colors enforced across every section... the constraint made every decision easier and every output more coherent.

**Typography:**

| Role | Font |
|------|------|
| Display / Headings | `Fraunces` - variable optical serif. Warm, editorial, literary. Not a startup font. |
| Body / UI | `Bricolage Grotesque` - quirky variable sans. Functional but never cold. |

**Icons:** Phosphor - Duotone weight throughout.

The two-tone effect picks up the teal perfectly against frosted glass cards.

## The Copy

Yeppp - every single word on this site was written from scratch. 🙃

Not "placeholder text replaced with real words."

Actually written. With a brand voice. With intention.

With the specific kind of person who lands on this page in mind.

The voice rule was simple: talk like a human who actually gets it.

Not a therapist reading from a manual.
Not a startup being inspirational.
Just honest, grounded, clear.

## The Images

Every image was prompted specifically for the brand...

Deep teal + mint white palette, photorealistic, cinematic. Generated via Adobe Firefly.

No generic stock photos.

Every prompt written to match the exact section, the exact emotional beat, the exact message it needed to carry.

Brand identity, UX design, and copywriting can all live in the same brain.

And honestly?? That's the only kind of website worth building. 🙃
